About the Club

Anyone, regardless of major or background, can join us

Everyone, regardless of major, background, etc. is welcome to join the club and work on their own game while surrounded by great peers and mentors. Game development is not only programming. We need artists, writers, musicians, sound technicians, designers, and more. You have to understand that not only is game development a very interdiscplinary field, but great game ideas come from having a diverse team

Our club doesn't work on 1 large game together. You get to work on your(and your friends') game alongside other developers

Our philosophy is that you will be driven by an unstoppable force when you get to work on your own game. We differentiate from other game development clubs in that we give you the freedom to pursue the game YOU want to build instead of working in a team on someone else's game. It means you get more freedom, but it also means you have to be more independent.

For the inexperienced, we have 2 different activities each year to help you get started

  1. During the fall semester, we'll be teaching the fundamentals through many series of workshops and club activities.
  2. During the spring semester, people who would like to work on a team with an experienced mentor can sign up for our annual game fair. Your goal would be to complete a demo of a game idea by the end of the semester

This semester we meet every Thursday from 6pm-8pm in LWSN B160

During our meetings, we'll most likely have people talk about the progress of their game, then go onto the activities for the inexperienced people. For the more veteran people, you can come here as a work place to meet up with other game developers, work on your game in a cool environment, or help us host the workshops/mentor teams for the inexperienced people.
